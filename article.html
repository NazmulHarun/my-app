The Six Pillars of Iman:
A Foundational Understanding

IA
Shaykh Ibrahim Al-Ansari Scholar of Aqeedah & Usul al-Fiqh

Bismillahi al-Rahmani al-Raheem. Faith in Islam is not merely a declaration of the tongue — it is a profound conviction of the heart, an affirmation of the intellect, and an expression in righteous deeds.

The Definition of Iman

Iman, in the Islamic tradition, is defined by scholars as: belief in the heart, declaration with the tongue, and action with the limbs. This definition unifies the dimensions of faith and prevents its reduction to mere ritual performance.

Hadith — Sahih Muslim

"Iman is to believe in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and to believe in divine decree — both its good and its harm."

Narrated by 'Umar ibn al-Khattab (may Allah be pleased with him)

The Six Pillars Explained

1. Belief in Allah (Tawheed)

The first and most essential pillar is belief in Allah — His existence, His oneness, His names, and His attributes. Scholars classify Tawheed into three categories: Rububiyyah (Lordship), Uluhiyyah (Worship), and Asma wa Sifat (Names & Attributes).

2. Belief in the Angels

Angels are created from light and are entirely obedient to Allah. Believing in them is obligatory, along with knowing the specific angels named in the Quran and Sunnah.

3. Belief in the Divine Books

Allah revealed books to His prophets as guidance for humanity. We believe in the Torah, the Psalms, the Gospel, and the final preserved revelation — the Noble Quran.

Indeed, it is We who sent down the Quran and indeed, We will be its guardian.

— Surah Al-Hijr, 15:9

4. Belief in the Prophets and Messengers

From Adam (peace be upon him) to Muhammad ﷺ, Allah sent prophets to every nation. We believe in all of them and hold that Muhammad ﷺ is the seal and final prophet.

5. Belief in the Last Day

The key events of the Last Day include:

  • The blowing of the Trumpet (Sur)
  • The resurrection of all souls
  • The presentation of deeds and the Scale (Mizan)
  • The Bridge over Hellfire (Sirat al-Mustaqeem)
  • Entry into Paradise (Jannah) or Hellfire (Jahannam)

6. Belief in Divine Decree (Qadar)

Qadar encompasses four levels: Allah's knowledge of all things, His recording in the Preserved Tablet, His will over all things, and His creation of all things. Believing in Qadar brings peace, patience in trial, and gratitude in blessing.

Practical Impact on Daily Life

  1. Belief in Allah cultivates constant mindfulness (muraqabah) and gratitude.
  2. Belief in angels encourages moral vigilance — knowing one is always observed.
  3. Belief in the books instils reverence for divine guidance and Quranic recitation.
  4. Belief in the messengers grounds faith in historical reality and righteous example.
  5. Belief in the Last Day sharpens accountability and long-term thinking.
  6. Belief in Qadar removes anxiety and replaces it with contentment (rida).

May Allah grant us firm and certain faith in all six pillars, and may He make us among those who live their beliefs. Ameen.
