Q

Is it permissible to perform Salah while wearing shoes?

+

Yes, it is permissible to pray with shoes on, provided the shoes are pure (tahir) and free from impurity. The Prophet ﷺ prayed in his shoes and also without them. Scholars consider it permissible and sometimes a Sunnah in open areas. Be mindful that the prayer area is not made impure and that it does not cause distraction in a mosque setting.

Fiqh Sh. Ibrahim Al-Ansari 15 Jan 2025
Q

What is the ruling on missing a day of fasting in Ramadan due to illness?

+

A person who misses fasting due to illness has a valid excuse and is not sinful. They must make up (qada) the missed days after Ramadan upon recovery. Allah says: "Whoever is ill or on a journey, then an equal number of other days" (2:185). If the illness is chronic with no hope of recovery, fidyah may be paid instead.

Fiqh Sh. Muhammad Al-Kurdi 10 Jan 2025
Q

How does one know if their sincere repentance (Tawbah) has been accepted?

+

Scholars mention several signs: (1) genuine change in the heart, (2) avoidance of the sin and its pathways, (3) increased fear of Allah, (4) the sin no longer holds appeal. Acceptance is ultimately known only to Allah. Combine hope in His mercy with continued striving and never fall into despair.

Spirituality Sh. Rania Al-Faris 8 Jan 2025
Q

What is the difference between Fard and Wajib in the Hanafi school?

+

In the Hanafi school, Fard is established by definitive evidence (qat'i) while Wajib is established by speculative evidence (zanni). Denying a Fard takes one out of Islam; denying a Wajib does not. Example: Salah is Fard; Witr is Wajib. This affects rulings in prayer, zakah, and hajj.

Fiqh Sh. Ahmad Al-Qasim 5 Jan 2025
